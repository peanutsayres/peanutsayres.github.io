What This Is

Accessus Publica provides temporary, paid access to professional-grade tools that are otherwise difficult to own, store, or justify full-time.

We start with one asset.

One geography.

One question.

This is not a marketplace.
This is not a studio.
This is not instruction or community programming.

This is access.

Phase 0 Pilot

Asset: 1 professional floor loom

Geography: Within ~100 miles of Toledo, Ohio

Duration: 90 days (hard stop)

Purpose:

To determine whether professional loom access generates repeat, paid utilization without owning a facility, vehicle fleet, or permanent inventory.

If it does, we proceed carefully.
If it does not, we stop.

No sunk-cost continuation.

The Loom

Professional floor loom (Macomber / equivalent)

  • Maintained and functional
  • Cleaned, inspected, insured
  • Not pristine
  • Not upgraded
  • Not bundled with supplies

This pilot tests access, not perfection.

How Access Works

Lease Terms (Pilot Only)

Minimum term: 30 days
Preferred: 60–90 days

Monthly pricing (pilot range):

$325–$375 — delivered
$275–$300 — customer pickup

Deposit: $500–$1,000 (based on lessee type)
Insurance: Held by Accessus (not renter-provided)

No hourly rentals.
No drop-ins.
No instruction.

Who This Is For

This pilot is intentionally narrow.

We are currently speaking with:

  • Weaving & fiber guilds
  • Universities and colleges
  • Institutional studios
  • Teaching weavers with overflow demand

If you need temporary access to a professional loom for real work — this may fit.

If you are looking for classes, shared studio space, or casual use — it will not.

Why Temporary Access

Professional looms require space, maintenance, and capital.

Temporary access means: no storage burden between projects, no equipment depreciation, no long-term commitment.

You use it. You return it. You move on.

What We’re Testing

This pilot is not about growth.
It’s about signal clarity.

We are measuring:

  • Price acceptance
  • Minimum term tolerance
  • Delivery vs pickup preference
  • Institutional vs guild demand
  • Logistics friction
  • Referral behavior

Revenue matters.
Learning matters more.

What This Is Not

To avoid confusion:

  • Not a teaching studio
  • Not a makerspace
  • Not a subscription service
  • Not a community program
  • Not a permanent facility

We do not scale this by accident.

Logistics (Phase 0)

  • Loom stored locally (climate-controlled)
  • Delivery via short-term vehicle rental
  • No owned vehicles
  • No fixed warehouse

If logistics outweigh value, the pilot ends.

What Happens After 90 Days

One of three outcomes:

If the pilot succeeds
  • Add 1–2 additional looms or adjacent fiber assets
  • Formalize referral pathways
  • Begin Phase 1 facility modeling (carefully)
If results are mixed
  • Adjust pricing, radius, or terms once
  • Extend pilot up to 60 days
  • Re-evaluate
If demand doesn’t materialize
  • We exit cleanly and document findings
  • Loom is sold or repurposed
  • Learnings shared for others exploring similar models

Stopping cleanly is a strength, not a failure.
